The App Experience

Set up once. Never plan a meal again.

The setup takes minutes and feels effortless. Underneath, Celeste is capturing everything she needs to run your household's food on autopilot: who's eating, your budget, your store, how you shop, and what your family actually likes. Here's exactly how it works, screen by screen.

01 — Setup

Set it up once. Never explain your household again.

Here's the difference that matters: general AI chatbots can spit out a meal plan, but you have to re-enter every allergy, every preference, and every dietary restriction every single time you ask. And then you're still left to build the grocery list and place the order yourself. With gourmai, your household profile is one and done. Celeste remembers everything, plans around it automatically, and handles the shopping, week after week, without you starting over.

The setup itself feels effortless. In a few guided screens, profiles build from a shared household template, so you only adjust what's different for each person. You can update any profile or household detail whenever life changes, but you'll never re-enter it from scratch.

  • One-and-done setup. Enter your household once, never repeat it
  • Up to 6 profiles, covering age, diets, health conditions & allergies
  • Your weekly grocery budget. Celeste plans to it and never exceeds your spend limit
  • Your grocery retailer. The stores you already use, pickup or delivery
  • Cuisine preferences, go-to dishes, cooking confidence & prep time
  • Update any profile or household detail anytime, on your terms

02 — Planning

Celeste plans it. But you're in control.

By default, Celeste plans a fresh week at a time. One base meal for the whole family, with smart modifications per person: your nut-allergic teen gets a safe version, your infant gets an unseasoned pull, your partner's heart-healthy swap is already in. Prefer fewer, bigger trips? Switch to 2-week planning anytime. It's an option, never a requirement.

And nothing is locked in. Don't love a suggestion? Swap any meal for whatever you want: paste a link to a recipe you found online, drop in a family heirloom recipe you've always made, or choose from gourmai's rotating featured meals. Celeste rebuilds your grocery list around your choice automatically. You never have to settle for exactly what she recommends.

Groceries handled. You always approve.

What changes by tier is how you interact: Autopilot and Household+ add voice & chat (manage the whole cart just by talking) and unlock the Professional Kitchen add-on.

  • You choose your order day and pickup or delivery, every week

From the Desk of Our Founder

gourmai didn't start as a startup. It started as a breaking point.

I'm a mother and a corporate executive. My weeks are built on back-to-back client deadlines, deliverables, and the kind of mental load that doesn't clock out when you walk through the door. There was one particular week. I don't think I need to describe it in detail, because if you're reading this, you probably know exactly what it felt like, where everything collided. Too many deadlines. Not enough hours. And somehow, on top of all of it, the weight of figuring out what to feed my family every single day.

It wasn't just the grocery run I didn't have time for. It was the mental tax of deciding. Planning. Remembering what everyone could and couldn't eat. Wondering if I had what I needed or if I needed to stop somewhere. And then cooking. Every day. Like a separate full-time job that nobody scheduled and everyone just expected.

I'd built my career helping organizations manage risk and complexity. I knew how to design systems that held up under pressure. But there was no system for this, for the daily operational burden of feeding a household when you are already running on empty.

So I built one. I built Celeste, an AI that learns your household like a family member would. She knows who has the nut allergy, who's heart-healthy, whose portion needs to be pulled before you season the pot. She plans the week. She builds the cart. She places the order. And on the weeks when you genuinely cannot do one more thing, she connects you to a licensed kitchen in your community that preps every meal and delivers it ready to heat.

This isn't about convenience for convenience's sake. It's about giving back something that was quietly being taken: the time and mental space to actually be present in your own home.

Laniece Holt
Founder & Chief Executive Officer · gourmai

Responsible AI

Technology that serves people and communities.

gourmai is built on powerful AI infrastructure. That comes with responsibility. Here is exactly how we think about it, across our product, our business model, and the communities we operate in.

Our AI Principles

Four things we will never compromise on.

i.

Privacy by Design

Your household data (dietary needs, health conditions, brand preferences) is never sold, never shared with advertisers, and never used to train models without explicit consent. Consent controls are built directly into the app, not buried in a terms-of-service page. You own your data. Full stop.

ii.

Transparent AI

Celeste tells you why she recommends what she recommends. No black boxes, no algorithmic manipulation disguised as helpfulness. You can always see the reasoning behind a meal plan, override any decision, and take back control. Transparency isn't a feature we built. It's a constraint we designed around.

iii.

Safety-First Health Logic

Allergen flags and health condition profiles hard-block incompatible meals. Not soft warnings, not suggestions to "check with your doctor." If a meal conflicts with any profile flag in your household, it simply does not appear. Celeste's health logic was designed as a protection system, not a recommendation engine.

iv.

Equitable Access

We priced gourmai deliberately. Even the Essential plan includes the core that matters most: autonomous meal planning and grocery fulfillment for your whole household. Higher tiers add conveniences like voice & chat and the Professional Kitchen add-on, but the intelligence that takes the daily food burden off your plate isn't reserved for the few. The planning engine that serves a Household+ family serves every Essential account too.

By the time dinner rolls around, you’re not lazy and you’re not disorganized. You’re depleted. You’ve spent the entire day making decisions — at work, for your kids, for your household — and the research on decision fatigue is clear: the quality and willpower behind our choices erodes the more of them we make.

What that means for dinner

Dinner lands at the exact moment your capacity to decide is at its lowest. So the “what’s for dinner” question — which also smuggles in “what do we have,” “what can everyone eat,” and “do I need to go to the store” — feels genuinely impossible. And the easiest exit is the most expensive one: ordering out, again, on money you didn’t plan to spend.

The goal isn’t more discipline at 6pm. It’s having no decision left to make.

Removing the decision entirely

This is the whole premise of gourmai. Celeste makes the dinner decisions earlier — when you set up your household once — and then keeps making them on autopilot. The plan is done. The groceries are handled. The question that used to drain you simply isn’t there anymore.

What you get back

Less impulse takeout and tighter grocery spend, yes. But the real return is mental space: the end of your day stops being one more shift of logistics, and starts being time you actually spend with the people you’re feeding.

Responsible AI

Why our allergen logic blocks meals instead of warning you.

How We Build4 min read

Most apps treat food allergies as a preference — a filter you can toggle, a warning label you can scroll past. When we designed Celeste’s health logic, we made a deliberate decision to do the opposite.

A protection system, not a suggestion engine

If a meal conflicts with an allergy or health flag on any profile in your household, it doesn’t show up with a caution icon. It doesn’t appear at all. The conflict is a hard block, not a soft warning — because a warning assumes you have the attention to read it, and we already know that by dinnertime, you might not.

Safety shouldn’t depend on the most tired person in the house noticing a warning label.

How it works in practice

  • Each profile carries its own allergies and health conditions, set once during setup.
  • Celeste checks every planned meal against every profile before it’s ever shown to you.
  • For infants, an unseasoned portion is pulled before seasoning — built into the plan, not left to memory.
  • Substitutions are never silent: if something changes, you’re told.

Why we hold this line

Responsible AI isn’t a marketing line for us — it’s a set of constraints we designed around. When the stakes are a child’s allergy, “probably fine” isn’t a standard we’re willing to ship.
